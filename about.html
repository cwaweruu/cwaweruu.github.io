About me

From Nairobi to Vilnius, building evidence that moves institutions

I am a Kenyan researcher and data analyst currently based in Vilnius, Lithuania. My work sits at the intersection of quantitative analysis, policy design, and institutional governance — a combination I arrived at not by accident, but through five years of working inside organisations where decisions affect real people at scale.

I began my career managing Kenya's national birth records database at the Ministry of Interior — the primary civil documentation system for the entire country. Then moved to health system data during a national rollout of universal health coverage across 12 counties. Those roles taught me something that a data science course cannot: that a clean dataset and a well-designed KPI framework are the difference between a government programme that works and one that only looks like it works on paper.

In 2023 I moved to Vilnius to complete a Postgraduate Diploma in Data Analytics at Turing College, followed by a certification in AI Fairness & Ethics. I joined Visionary Analytics as a researcher, where I have since contributed to four published studies for EU institutions — the European Parliament, the EESC, and UNI Europa — covering digital sovereignty, green transition policy, workers' health, and labour market governance.

I am committed to building AI and data governance systems that serve African communities, informed by both African institutional realities and European regulatory experience. That combination — Nairobi and Vilnius, policy and code, governance and data — is not incidental. It is the work.
