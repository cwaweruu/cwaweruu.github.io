Research & Publications

Four publications with EU institutions

Produced as a junior researcher at Visionary Analytics, Vilnius. Each report was commissioned by a European institution and is publicly available. My contributions spanned quantitative analysis, econometric modelling, desk research, stakeholder interviews, and policy writing.

EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT 2025 Published

European Software & Cyber Dependencies

Assessment of European entities' dependence on non-EU software ecosystems and associated regulatory and security risks. Covers digital sovereignty, public procurement vulnerabilities, and governance implications for EU AI infrastructure.

My contributions

  • Market analysis of EU public procurement data in the software sector
  • Key informant interviews with stakeholders across EU Member States
  • Desk research on regulatory and security risk frameworks
  • Drafted sections on digital sovereignty and governance implications

Directly relevant to EU AI governance debates and the regulation of critical digital infrastructure.

EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT 2025 Published

Effects of Extreme Weather Conditions on Workers' Health and Safety

Occupational health and safety risks across EU economic sectors, examining gaps in existing EU and Member State legislation and offering structured recommendations for regulatory reform.

My contributions

  • Cross-sector data analysis of occupational exposure and health outcomes
  • Regulatory gap analysis comparing EU and Member State legislation
  • Evidence synthesis and structured policy recommendations
  • Contributed to published sections on the legislative review

Demonstrates ability to identify regulatory blind spots and translate evidence into concrete governance recommendations.

EESC 2026 Published

Socially Just Transition in the Green Deal: Cost of Inaction

Social and employment impacts of green and digital transitions across 7 EU Member States. Models employment, poverty, and regional disparity outcomes to 2045 under three policy scenarios.

My contributions

  • Scenario analysis and econometric modelling of transition outcomes to 2045
  • Partial-equilibrium modelling comparing BAU, weak, and strong JTD scenarios
  • Policy case studies assessing employment, poverty, and regional disparities across 7 Member States
  • Visualised quantitative impacts for policymaker audiences

Bridges digital transformation, data governance, and evidence-based social policy at continental scale.

UNI EUROPA 2025 Published

Overview Report on Collective Bargaining Challenges in Service Sectors

Collective bargaining practices and worker participation rates across EU service sectors, with targeted recommendations for policymakers and trade unions on structural reform.

My contributions

  • Comparative analysis of collective bargaining coverage data across EU Member States
  • Assessment of structural barriers to worker representation in service sectors
  • Policy recommendations for policymakers and trade unions
  • Contributed to the comparative framework design

Illustrates experience translating workers' rights and governance analysis into actionable institutional recommendations.

All four publications were produced during my time as a Junior Researcher at Visionary Analytics, Vilnius, where I led or contributed to EU-funded research tenders commissioned by European institutions. The work involved mixed methods: quantitative modelling, qualitative case studies, key informant interviews, and structured policy writing reviewed by senior economists and legal experts.
