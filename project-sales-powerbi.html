Regional Revenue & Market Performance Dashboard

The Problem

A business operating across multiple regions had no consolidated view of revenue performance or growth KPIs. Regional managers worked from separate reports built on different assumptions, with no common baseline. Resource allocation decisions were driven by seniority and advocacy — not evidence. The goal was a single executive dashboard enabling cross-regional comparison and identifying where investment would have the highest impact.

The Approach

I consolidated multi-regional sales data in Power BI, first standardising currency, date formats, and regional naming conventions across source files — and documenting every alignment decision so the business understood the assumptions underpinning the numbers.

I built DAX measures for: revenue by region; period-over-period growth rates; market penetration as a percentage of addressable market; and ROI by marketing channel. The final dashboard used a consistent layout across all regions so comparison was visually immediate rather than requiring cognitive effort.

Key Findings

Two regions were consistently underperforming relative to their addressable market size — suggesting an execution problem, not a market problem. The gap was invisible before the dashboard because those regions were measured only against their own prior periods.

Marketing ROI varied significantly by channel: the highest-spend channel was not the highest-return channel — a gap that directly changed the quarterly budget allocation.

Revenue concentration was high: two regions contributed a disproportionate share of total revenue, creating organisational risk that was invisible in the regional-only view each manager previously used.

Impact

The dashboard directly informed a resource reallocation discussion at the executive level. The ROI gap between marketing channels was immediately actionable: the next quarter's budget was adjusted before the dashboard had been live for two weeks.

What I Learned

The hardest part of a regional dashboard is not the DAX — it's convincing regional stakeholders that a common metric definition is more valuable than their local customisations. Every region had a reason why their numbers should be measured differently. But a dashboard that can be interpreted differently by each viewer is not a dashboard; it's a set of separate reports with shared colours. Alignment on definitions precedes alignment on strategy.
